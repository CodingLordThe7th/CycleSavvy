Cycling Safety Tips

  • Wear a Helmet: Always wear a properly fitted helmet. It significantly reduces the risk of serious head injuries in case of accidents.
  • Be Visible: Use front and rear lights and wear reflective gear. Drivers often overlook cyclists, so visibility ensures you’re seen day and night.
  • Check Your Bike: Brakes, tires, and chain should be inspected before every ride. Mechanical failures at high speed can cause accidents and leave you stranded.
  • Follow the Rules: Ride with traffic, obey signs, and use hand signals. This builds trust with drivers and prevents collisions at intersections.
  • Stay Alert: Watch for cars, pedestrians, and road hazards. Quick reactions to potholes, doors opening, or sudden stops can save your life.
  • Carry Essentials: Bring water, ID, and a repair kit. Being prepared keeps you hydrated, safe, and able to fix small breakdowns.
  • Use Bike Lanes: Stick to designated cycling lanes whenever possible. They’re designed to give you more space and protection from cars.
  • Control Your Speed: Slow down in crowded areas and intersections. Riding too fast limits reaction time and increases stopping distance.
  • Make Eye Contact: Ensure drivers see you before crossing or turning. Eye contact reduces misunderstandings and prevents dangerous moves by cars.
  • Avoid Headphones: Keep your ears free. You need to hear honks, sirens, or approaching vehicles to react in time.
